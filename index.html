Chinese History Timeline



Ancient China is the oldest and longest lasting empire in human history. It began with the Qin dynasty and the first emperor Qin who united all of China under one rule in 221 BC. Ancient China's history can be more than 2000 years! The rich culture of ancient China is still evident in modern China. Farming villages gave rise to dynasties like the Zhou (1046–256 BCE), Qin (221-206 BCE), and Ming (1368-1644 C.E.).

1600 – 1046 BCE

1046–221 BCE
Zhou Dynasty
Confucianism emerged


Despite the rise and fall of numerous dynasties and occasional times of instability, Imperial China was remarkably stable and had a sophisticated political structure that feutured the idea of a meritocracy. Imperial China also produced many technological innovations, such as paper and porcelain, that transformed the world and we still use today. Imperial China spanned for more than 2,000 years (221 BCE - CE 1912).

Prior to the migration of its central government to Taiwan as a result of the Chinese Civil War, the Republic of China (ROC), an East Asian nation with its capital on Mainland China from 1912 to 1949, was established in Mainland China. It was the most inhabited nation in the world in 1949 with a population of 541 million people.
1912–1949 CE
Republic of China


The People's Republic of China was established on October 1st 1949. Currently it has more inhabitants than any other country in the world, more than 1.4 billion people! Since the establishment of the People’s Republic of China in 1949, the CCP (Chinese Communist Party) has been in sole control of that country’s government.
1949–present CE
